Bangalore

The city I grew up in...

Bangalore is the capital of the Indian State of Karnataka. It is nicknamed the Garden City and was once called a Pensioner's Paradise. Due to its high elevation, Bangalore usually enjoys a more moderate climate throughout the year.

This cosmolpotian city gave me a wonderful childhood, adolescence and adulthood, filled with new experiences and old favorites. The highlights of my 24 years in the city are:

The Sights

Though I grew up in a bustling city, Bangalore never made me feel like I was in a concrete jungle. I have happy memories of visiting the National Park with my family to watch exotic animals. We've had gatherings with the extended family (that ranged four generations) in Cubbon Park where we'd spend the day playing cricket, badminton, frisbee and devouring the delcious food packed for the picnic. I've had the fortune to experience the breathtaking experience of the view from Nandi Hills with my friends.

The Landmarks

Vidhana Soudha is the seat of State Legislature of Karnataka with a beautiful dravidian style of architecture making it a popular tourist attraction and a significant landmark. The Banaglore Palace was purchased from Rev. Garrett in 1873 AD by the British Guardians for minor Maharaja Chamarajendra Wadiyaris education and adminstrative training. It is also where my best friend got married, leaving me with a lifetime of fond memories attached to this structure. IISc is the premier institute for advanced scientific and technological research and education in India and continues to be a source of inspiration to all those who share the city with this elite institution.

The IT Boom

Bangalore has rapidly evolved from a quiet city to becoming India's Silicon Valley. It attracts the best minds from the country and the world. I was fortunate to experience the work culture in some of these gorgeous tech parks.

The After Hours

Bangalore was one of the earliest cities that provided a platform for upcoming local artists. Attending their shows from an early stage makes us feel like a part of their journey, many of whom have now been caterpulted to stardom. Bangalore has a vibrant music scene equally attracting both local and international artists. There are also an excellent selection of craft beers available in any of the innumerable microbreweries in the city.

The Local Classics

Bangalore has many iconic eateries that have seen the city through its remarkable transformation. My favorite is CTR, which serves the most scrumptious butter masala dosa, bajji & coffee.The street food is an adventure by itself, especially the iftar stalls that pop up on Mosque road during Ramzan. But if there's one thing that is synonmous with home, it is the ice-cream from Corner House that serves the richest, most luscious chocolate sauce.
